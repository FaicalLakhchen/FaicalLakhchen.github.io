About

Ph.D. in Applied Economics | Researcher in Macro-Finance, DSGE Modeling & Development Economics.

I hold a Ph.D. in Applied Economics from Ibn Zohr University, where I also teach (Part time) courses in macroeconomics and quantitative methods. My research focuses on the intersection of macroeconomics and finance—macro-finance—with a particular emphasis on developing and emerging economies. I study how financial frictions shape the business cycle, using Dynamic Stochastic General Equilibrium (DSGE) models to better understand the transmission of shocks and the role of monetary policy.

In parallel, I explore labor market dynamics, examining how employment, wages, and policy interact under structural constraints often present in low- and middle-income countries. My approach blends theory with applied macroeconometrics, always aiming to connect rigorous analysis with real-world policy relevance.

This commitment to research that bridges academic insight and policy application was recognized when I was awarded the Bank Al-Maghrib Prize for Economic and Financial Research (2025), for my work on macro-financial dynamics in the Moroccan economy.
